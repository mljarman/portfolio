Animal Adoption

Solo Project

A one week project that resulted in a Plotly dash app that predicts the probability that a dog or cat will be adopted.

Data was pulled from the Austin Animal Shelter in Texas. While the initial data included different types of animals including livestock, I decided to narrow it down to only dogs and cats. After exploratory data analysis and data cleaning, a random forest classifier was used based on numerous features of the animals including breed, color, age, sex, and whether or not the animal was fixed. This last feature proved to be the most significant to the model.

In the Plotly Dash app, users are able to customize features of a particular animal and a probability is returned on the animal being adopted or not. I learned about Uplift Modeling at this time and was wondering the possiblities of using it in the case of sheltered animals. Perhaps those whose predicted probability of being adopted is low could be helped with marketing. A spotlight story on them through social media that may get potential pet owners more interested in those less desirable animals.
